GIS Taiwan 2009 / Call for Participation

Eligibility

The essay competition is open to graduates and undergraduates, yet applicants must not be born prior to the year of 1974. All participants should be enrolled in an accredited college or university prior to 1st February, 2009, with proof of academic enrollment, either in the form of a transcript (with the participant's name and official stamp of his/her school) or a formal Certificate of Academic Enrollment. Any application made without proof of academic enrollment will be automatically disqualified. 80 participants will be admitted to the conference.

Author Anonymity

The applicant's name must only appear on the application form. Related information of the author must not appear in the abstract, and in the essay text in any form.

Format

Participants may only submit one essay on one of the three subtopics provided., and they can choose a subtopic to interpret. All entries must abide by the following rules:

  • Abstract should not exceed 300 words of text;
  • Essay should not exceed 2,000 words of text;
  • All essays must be written in English;
  • Essays must be submitted in A4 format, with left and right margins both set at 2.5cm;
  • The title line must be in 16 point- Times New Roman;
  • The content must be in 12 point- Times New Roman;
  • All papers must be submitted digitally as .PDF documents;
  • The file name of the abstract must be A10001 (PIN);
  • The file name of the essay must be E10001 (PIN);
  • Handwritten essays or essays presented through any other method will be automatically disqualified;
  • A limited number of charts or tables may be used. Equations should be avoided as much as possible and, if used, must be confined to an appendix or footnote;
  • All pages should be numbered in the lower right-hand corner;
  • Word count and PIN should be disclosed at the beginning of first page, in the upper left-hand corner. EX: Word count: 1,899/PIN: 10001.

Deadline

All applicants must be registered to the GIS Taiwan 2009 website. All essays must be uploaded by 23:59 GMT, 1st April, 2009 to be eligible for the competition.

Originality

Essays must be original, factually accurate, and must not violate the law, nor infringe the equitable right of any person or entity.

Citation

Participants must not commit plagiarism in any form. Where appropriate, participants are encouraged to conduct further individual research by reading relevant books and articles and/or by making use of the Internet. It is expected that participants cite all quotations by means of any one of the following international formal citation methods:

  1. The Chicago Manual of Style (abbr. in writing as CMS or CMOS, or verbally as Chicago) ; http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/
  2. MLA Style Manual and Guide to Scholarly Publishing; http://www.mla.org/style
  3. American Psychological Association (APA) style. http://www.apastyle.org/

Terms and Copyrights

All submitted materials will be free of restrictions and limitations in their use, reproduction, and publication. All essays become the property of GIS Taiwan upon submission, along with their copyright. GIS Taiwan reserves the right to use, edit, and excerpt these entries for promotional and/or any other purpose, including posting them online without compensation.

Attention

All participants of GIS Taiwan 2009 must take full responsibility for their essays. By submitting an essay, each participant acknowledges his or her understanding of and compliance with rules mentioned above. GIS Taiwan 2009 reserves the right to disqualify any entry deemed inappropriate for publication, at its discretion.

Criteria for Evaluation

An independent judging panel will review all submitted essays. Each essay will be evaluated on its own merit by a group of experts representing, among others, the academic and the business sector. A first round selection will be conducted to eliminate essays which do not meet the GIS Taiwan 2009 basic evaluation criteria.

Qualified-level entries must be well-written, thorough and concise. Entries will be judged on how well participants have focused their ideas and how well they have addressed the following basic criteria:

  • Analysis and logic of argument;
  • Topic selection and feasibility;
  • Significance and innovation;
  • Organization and structure.

Announcement of results

Qualified participants will be notified via e-mail on 25 April 2009 (by 23:59 GMT time). 

Contacts
